Fast fashion

The textile industry is often considered one of the most polluting sectors, contributing to around 10% of greenhouse gas emissions and significant water pollution. Unfortunately, about 85% of textiles end up discarded in piles like this

Most fast fashion clothes are produced in places where workers often face low wages, poor working conditions, and sometimes even child labor

Here are some things to have in mind, to help achieve the UN's goal of reducing waste:

  • Take care of your clothes by washing them properly and repairing any damages.
  • Buy clothes with quality materials to ensure longevity.
  • When possible, buy second-hand clothing to give items a second life and reduce demand for new production.
  • Only buy when you truly need something, avoiding impulse purchases and unnecessary consumption.
  • Consider participating in clothing swaps or renting clothes for special occasions to reduce the need for new purchases.
  • Support brands that prioritize sustainability and ethical practices, such as using eco-friendly materials, reducing carbon emissions, and ensuring fair wages for workers.
